Molecular orbital theory

Last week I held a tutorial for undergraduate chemistry students about molecular orbital theory. I really enjoyed going over the fundamental principles on quantum chemistry, and as such wanted to write a short post on the basics.

Molecular orbital theory is a somewhat crude approximation to allow us to calculate the energy of an electron in an orbital, forming the basis for calculating the energy of a molecule using, for example, the self-consistent field procedure.
